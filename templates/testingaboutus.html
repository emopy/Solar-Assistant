Why do solar panels need to be cleaned?

Over time, solar panels experience something called soiling. Soiling is essentially the build-up of debris on the surface of the solar panel modules, effecting the efficiency of the entire solar farm. Using a specific formula, you calcute the expected energy output of a solar farm on any given date. This is calcuated using the farm's size (kWp), insolation, average module temp, and the temperature coefficent of the site. kWp * insolation * (1+ ((avg temp C - 25) * (Temp Coef))

As the solar farm continue use and debris begins to build-up the efficiency decreases as the modules can not work at peak preformance. Three different solar farm sites were analyzed based on efficiency over time:

The efficiency of each site was calculated by taking the actual yield of each site on a given day and dividing that by the expected yield from the formula. Using this data, you can visualize the decrease and spikes of efficiency as the solar farm is used.

In addition to this, the overall value of the solar farm and the cost to clean each site can be measured. For New jersey, the value of each kWh is $0.25 and the cost to clean modules is $5 per kWp. Using both of these values, the optimal time to clean your solar farm can be calculated.
